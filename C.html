C.

C. is a shopping app for apparel. It has a variety of brands for users to choose from, and a large catalog for users to explore and find the item to complete their look. C. stands for charisma and character. How a person dresses defines their character. Different styles create different personas, and that is what C. embodies. Each style and clothing piece brings out a charismatic charm, and that is the essence of the app.

Competitor Analysis

For my competitors, I picked two large scale companies that I frequently shop with. These two competitors gave me a lot of inspiration on how I wanted my app to look and feel. I really liked the large selection of clothes they have, and the presentation of their products.

The luxurious aesthetic was the direction I wanted my app to take. It was important to see how my competitors achieved the aesthetic, without sacrificing any of the functions and features.

I spend a good amount of time shopping and discovering new apparel and fashion styles. That is why I really wanted to work on this project. It was something I was already familiar with. The next step was to interview other users and see what needs could be fulfilled with my app's features.

User Personas

User personas helped provide other perspectives. Learning about the different frustrations of users was vital in creating an app that would help solve those problems.

Finding the common goals was another vital part in desigining the app. Common goals helped me understand what features were necessary across multiple platforms.

User Flow

User flows helped me organize what tabs and categories I wanted to include in my app.

With clothing apps, there are a lot of categories and it could be overwhelming. That is why I only wanted to include the most popular ones, and organize it on the flow diagram.

The user flows also helped me highlight the important tabs and functions that users need. It emphasizes the features that would help users solve their problems and alleviate their frustrations.

Low-Fidelity Screens

I wanted to include the low-fidelity screens to show the entire process. For the low-fi screens, I used Balsamiq to draft out how the app would be organized, and where the buttons would be positioned.

The sketches serve as a skeleton for my app, and it really helped me visualize the foundation of the design.

Mid-Fidelity Screens

With the low-fi screens created, it was time to start creating the visual aspect of the app. It was important to start piecing together how the app would look, and how I would organize each page. This process was the most fun but also the most time consuming. It was important to get the organization right and as polished as possible before adding the colors and pictures to finalize the designs.
